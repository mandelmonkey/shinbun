Oil prices fell sharply in Asian trading after hopes emerged that the United States, Israel and Iran could move toward a broader peace agreement. Brent crude dropped below 98 dollars a barrel, with markets focused on the possible reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, effectively closed since late February. Analysts caution that energy markets may remain tight as global stockpiles have been depleted and damaged facilities will take time to recover.

RAF jet carrying UK defence secretary jammed near Russian border

An RAF aircraft carrying UK Defence Secretary John Healey had its GPS signal jammed while flying back from Estonia near the Russian border. The pilots switched to an alternative navigation system during the three-hour flight, and Russia is believed to be behind the interference. The incident highlights growing military tension along NATO's eastern flank.

UK education secretary seeks probe into hidden childcare costs

Deposits, meal fees, nappy charges and other add-ons are undermining the promise of free hours and creating barriers for families under financial pressure. Japan-linked tanker completes first Hormuz transit since closure

Major convenience store chains cut deliveries amid fuel price surge

Major Japanese convenience store chains are reducing delivery frequency as fuel costs linked to the Iran conflict put pressure on logistics. FamilyMart will cut deliveries from three to two per day at about 1,300 stores starting in June, while Seven-Eleven Japan has already made similar reductions in some areas. The companies hope the changes will control costs without passing higher prices on to shoppers.

Voice actor sues over unauthorised AI voice cloning on TikTok

Voice actor Tsuda Kenjiro has filed a lawsuit seeking the removal of TikTok videos that he says used generative AI to imitate his voice without permission.
