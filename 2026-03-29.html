Japan's major shipping firms halt all Hormuz Strait transit

Japan's largest shipping companies have fully suspended transit through the Strait of Hormuz. With over 90% of Japan's crude oil imports coming from the Middle East, a prolonged disruption could have severe economic consequences. The government is reportedly considering countermeasures including the release of strategic petroleum reserves.
