US and Iran agree two-week ceasefire; Strait of Hormuz to reopen

The United States and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire on Wednesday, with Tehran's foreign minister stating that the Strait of Hormuz would be open for safe navigation during the truce. Pakistan, which brokered the deal, said talks aimed at ending the conflict would begin in Islamabad on April 10. International oil prices fell sharply on the news, with WTI futures briefly dropping below $92 per barrel.

Oil prices plunge as US-Iran ceasefire eases energy market fears

International oil prices fell sharply following the US-Iran ceasefire agreement, with Brent crude declining significantly after weeks of elevated prices caused by the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz. Airlines had been cutting flights and raising fares due to soaring jet fuel costs, and household energy bills across the UK and beyond had risen sharply. Markets welcomed the news as a potential turning point for global energy supplies.

Nikkei surges over 2,800 points on US-Iran ceasefire news

Tokyo's Nikkei 225 index surged by more than 2,800 points on Wednesday after the announcement of the US-Iran ceasefire eased fears over prolonged energy price inflation. Investors had been anxiously watching the conflict's impact on oil supplies, and the relief rally swept across transport, aviation, and manufacturing stocks. Three workers killed in fall at Kawasaki steelworks

Three workers were killed and one remains missing after five men fell from height at a steelworks in Kawasaki on Tuesday. Police believe a counterweight attached to a crane being dismantled came loose, causing the workers to fall along with it. Authorities are continuing to search for the missing worker while investigators examine the circumstances of the accident. The incident has prompted renewed scrutiny of safety standards in heavy industrial workplaces.
