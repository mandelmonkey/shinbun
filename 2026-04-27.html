UK's largest environmental pollution case opens in High Court

A landmark collective lawsuit over pollution of the Rivers Wye, Lugg and Usk has opened at London's High Court, with more than 4,500 local residents and workers joining the claim. Campaigners allege that manure from intensive poultry farming and sewage spills have caused algal blooms and a sharp decline in river health. The defendants, Avara Foods and Welsh Water, deny the allegations, but the case could reshape environmental accountability in Britain.

Healthy life expectancy gap between rich and poor widens to 20 years

A new report from the Health Foundation reveals that people in England's wealthiest areas can expect around 20 more years of good health than those in the poorest areas. Average healthy life expectancy across the UK has also fallen by roughly two years over the past decade, placing the country near the bottom among comparable high-income nations. Researchers point to deprivation, obesity, poor housing, mental illness and pandemic aftereffects as key drivers.
