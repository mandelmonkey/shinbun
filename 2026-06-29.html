First Antarctic dinosaur bone found — in a museum drawer since 1985

Palaeontologists have identified the first dinosaur bone ever found in Antarctica, which had been sitting unnoticed in a museum drawer since it was collected in 1985. The fossil is part of the tail of a Titanosaur, one of the largest dinosaurs to have lived. The discovery opens new possibilities for understanding how dinosaurs spread across the ancient supercontinent Gondwana.
