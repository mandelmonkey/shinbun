Plaid Cymru becomes largest party in Welsh Parliament

Plaid Cymru has become the largest party in the Welsh Parliament, ending Labour's roughly century-long dominance in Wales. The party is framing the result as a historic shift and argues it now has a mandate to lead the Welsh government. The outcome also feeds a broader debate about whether support for the UK's traditional major parties is weakening.

SNP wins Scottish election as Reform and Labour tie for second

John Swinney's SNP finished as the leading party in the Scottish election but fell short of an outright majority. The more striking result was the tie for second place between Reform and Labour, suggesting an increasingly fragmented political landscape. Together with the Welsh and English results, it reinforces the sense that British politics is entering a less predictable multi-party phase.

Great Western Railway to be renationalised within months

Great Western Railway, the operator linking London with South Wales and the West of England, is set to come under government control. The move gives Labour a concrete example of public ownership in the rail sector.
