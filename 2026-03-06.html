Japanese startup Space One aborts Kairos No.3 rocket just 68 seconds after launch

Japanese space startup Space One launched its Kairos No. 3 rocket from the Spaceport Kii private launch facility in Kushimoto, Wakayama, on Thursday, but the mission was aborted 68 seconds after liftoff when the autonomous flight termination system activated at an altitude of around 30 kilometres. It was the company's third consecutive failed launch. President Masakazu Toyoda apologised and reaffirmed the company's goal of 20 missions per year by the late 2020s.
